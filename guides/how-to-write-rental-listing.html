How to write a rental listing that gets serious tenants

By the chennairents.in Editorial Team

When listing a flat online without a broker, your listing description acts as your salesperson. Vague descriptions lead to irrelevant phone calls and wasting time on site visits. Drafting a descriptive, honest listing helps filter inquiries so you only talk to highly interested prospects.

1. Be Specific About Financial Breakdowns

Always state the exact monthly rent and security deposit. If maintenance charges are not included in the rent, state them clearly (e.g., "Rent: ₹25,000 + Maintenance: ₹3,500"). Transparency prevents awkward negotiations later and ensures the tenant has the budget to support your pricing.

2. Note the Key Infrastructure Details

Tenants in Chennai look for specific logistical guarantees: - **Water Supply:** Explicitly state if you have municipal Metrowater, borewell, or if you rely on tanker deliveries. - **Power Backup:** Note if you have a UPS backup, full generator backup (DG), or no backup. - **Parking:** Clarify if there is dedicated covered car parking, open parking, or only bike parking.

3. Outline Your House Policies Upfront

If you have specific preferences regarding food habits (e.g., veg only), tenant type (bachelors, families), or pets, state them. While keeping options open increases inquiries, setting clear policies upfront saves time for both you and the prospective renters.
