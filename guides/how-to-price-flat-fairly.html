How to price your flat fairly using tenant-reported rent data

By the chennairents.in Editorial Team

One of the biggest mistakes landlords in Chennai make is pricing their properties based on broker-quoted online values. High asking prices lead to prolonged vacancies, costing you far more than a slight adjustment in monthly rent. We look at how to use actual market data to find the optimal rental price for your property.

1. Calculate the Real Cost of Vacancy

If your flat remains vacant for two months because you are holding out for a rent of ₹28,000 instead of accepting the market rate of ₹25,000, you lose ₹50,000 in immediate income. To recover that lost ₹50,000 at the higher rate, it will take you over 16 months. Pricing your property correctly from day one ensures it is occupied immediately, maximizing your net annual yield.

2. Search Local Tenant-Reported Averages

Standard listing sites show asking rents, but ChennaiRents shows what tenants are *actually* paying. Look at the pins in your immediate locality. If the average rent for a standalone 2BHK in your street is ₹22,000, setting your price at ₹23,000 is reasonable; setting it at ₹27,000 will alienate serious seekers.

3. Premium Adjustments

You can command a premium above the local average only if you offer specific features: - **Amenities:** Dedicated covered car parking (not just open or bike parking) is worth a ₹2,000 premium. - **Power Backup:** Full DG backup in gated complexes adds substantial value on OMR. - **Metro Access:** Proximity to CMRL Blue or Green line stations allows a premium of 10-15%.

Set a Realistic Advance Deposit

While the standard in Chennai is a 10-month advance, this acts as a major barrier for quality tenants. Consider offering a 5-6 month advance option; this makes your property highly attractive and lets you select from a wider pool of prospective tenants.
